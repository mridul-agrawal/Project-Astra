## BEAT 1 — The Hunt  *(STILL + Narrator; calm, mythic)*

*Still: a young hunter moving low through the dusk jungle, bow in hand, eyes on the ground — reading a trail. Not aiming at anything; tracking.*

- Narrator — "Below the mountains, where the jungle keeps its oldest secrets, there was a village that asked little of the world."
- Narrator — "And a girl who hunted its forests — who could read a trail like scripture, and loose an arrow before a deer thought to run."
- Narrator — "This dusk, she tracked alone, the way she liked best."

## BEAT 2 — The Trail  *(STILL + Aranya's voice; investigating, unease)*

*Still: she crouches over the forest floor — deep, clawed prints, too large, gouged into the earth, running in a straight line off through the trees.*

- Aranya *(focused — a hunter reading it)* — "Fresh tracks. Still soft at the edges."
- Aranya *(puzzled)* — "But these aren't deer. Not boar. …Nothing I've ever tracked."
- Aranya *(uneasy)* — "Too deep. Clawed. And they don't wander like a beast — they go straight."
- Aranya *(dawning dread)* — "…Straight for the village."

## BEAT 3 — The Fire  *(STILL + Aranya's voice; alarm)*

*Still: she straightens, follows the trail's line with her eyes toward home — and beyond the treeline, a column of black smoke and firelight climbs the dusk sky.*

- Aranya *(breath catching)* — "…Smoke. Over the rooftops."
- Aranya *(alarmed)* — "The village is burning. That's *home*—"

*(She runs.)*

## BEAT 4 — The Reveal  *(high-intensity STILL + her reaction)*

*Bespoke full-screen still: the village edge — homes ablaze, smoke, fallen villagers in the lanes, two Rakshasas advancing through it.*

- Aranya *(anguished)* — "No…"
- Aranya *(anguished)* — "The lanes. The fallen. And those things moving through it all."
- Aranya *(determined)* — "Rakshasas. …So *that* was the trail."
- Aranya *(determined)* — "Not one more. They take no one else."

## BEATS 5–7 — The Child  *(PORTRAIT; Aranya left, Child right; village backdrop)*

*The conversation that carries the exposition, the temple reveal, the priority call, and the gatekeeper thread.*

- Child *(terrified)* `[right]` — "Aranya! Aranya—!"
- Aranya *(determined)* `[left]` — *(kneeling)* "I'm here. Breathe. Look at me — what happened?"
- Child *(terrified)* `[right]` — "Monsters — they came out of the trees. They burned the granary, and Maa told me to run and not look back—"
- Aranya *(anguished)* `[left]` — "You did right to run. Where are the others?"
- Child *(pleading)* `[right]` — "Hiding. Some… didn't."
- Child *(pleading)* `[right]` — "And they're at the temple too. They want what's inside it — the priest is guarding it."
- Aranya *(alarmed)* `[left]` — *(quiet)* "The temple…"
- Aranya *(determined)* `[left]` — "But the bleeding is *here*, in these lanes. I hold them here first. The temple after."
- Aranya *(determined)* `[left]` — "Gajen — the gatekeeper — where is he?"
- Child *(pleading)* `[right]` — "At the temple. Fighting. Losing. He can't hold them alone."
- Aranya *(determined)* `[left]` — "Then he won't be alone for long."
- Aranya *(determined)* `[left]` — "Hide — and don't come out until it's quiet. Promise me."
- Child *(pleading)* `[right]` — "…Promise."

## Notes for revision

- **Why the hunter opening works:** showing her *tracking with a bow* establishes "trained archer" without a word of exposition — and the **monstrous trail** seeds the Rakshasas as dread before we ever see one, then pays off when she connects it at the reveal ("So *that* was the trail").
- **Why fire, not screams:** she's far from home — she can't plausibly hear screams, and a scream can't be drawn. A column of smoke/fire is visual, logical, and instantly readable as "home is in danger."
- **Narrator handoff:** the storyteller sets the scene in Beat 1, then steps back — from Beat 2 we're inside Aranya's head as she works the trail and sees the fire, and her first *dialogue* (to another person) is with the child. Narration for the world; her voice for the danger.
- **Stills needed (per the brief's asset list):** Beat 1 hunter-tracking, Beat 2 monstrous-tracks (can be a tight detail shot, possibly folded into Beat 1's still), Beat 3 fire-over-the-treeline, Beat 4 village-under-attack (the high-intensity one). Her *portrait* (for dialogue) is only needed from Beat 5 on — the opening is stills, so fewer portrait expressions are needed than v1 implied.
- **Tone guardrails:** Narrator = mythic but plain (epic in imagery, not archaic diction); Aranya = courageous but human; the Child = breathless, fragmentary.
- **Open (brief §4):** confirm Option A; final names; how many of Beats 1–4 are distinct stills vs combined; whether the woods opening is purely narrated stills or a tiny playable moment.
