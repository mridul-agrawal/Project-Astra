GAME: Project Astra — a tactical RPG (Fire Emblem–style grid combat) set in the era of ancient India's epics, the Mahabharata and Ramayana. The game pairs pixel-art gameplay with detailed, hand-illustrated character portraits in the modern Fire Emblem tradition.

USE: A character DIALOGUE PORTRAIT (a mugshot / bust) for the heroine, shown beside her lines in cutscenes and conversations. Head-and-shoulders, expressive, on a plain/dark background so it can be cleanly cut out and placed in the dialogue UI. This is her neutral BASE expression (other expressions — alarmed, anguished, determined — will be made as variants of this same face/design).

CHARACTER: "Aranya" — a young woman, late teens to early twenties, from a small jungle village in ancient India. She is a FOREST HUNTER and ARCHER — not royalty, a self-taught local warrior. Distinctly Indian features: warm brown skin, dark expressive eyes, long dark hair worn in a practical braid with a few loose windblown strands. Ragged, practical, earth-toned attire — worn cloth and leather, a simple forest-suited wrap/tunic, perhaps a quiver strap across one shoulder and small tribal beadwork; NOT ornate or royal. Lean, weathered, capable. Expression: calm but resolute — courageous, alert, a touch youthful; someone at home in the wild and ready for it.

STYLE: A detailed, HD, hand-illustrated character portrait in the modern Fire Emblem portrait tradition (think Fire Emblem Awakening / Fates / Three Houses key art) — clean lineart with soft painterly shading, high detail, warm cinematic lighting. Explicitly NOT low-resolution GBA-style pixel art — smooth and HD. Cohesive ancient-India palette: warm earth tones, deep forest greens, golden light.

FRAMING / POSE (match the Fire Emblem GBA mug convention): a head-and-shoulders bust in a THREE-QUARTER view — body and head turned about 30–45° to one side (canonically toward the viewer's RIGHT, as in FE GBA portraits), shoulders angled with the turn, and her eyes looking toward that same side (toward an off-frame conversation partner). NOT front-on, NOT staring at the camera, and NOT a full profile — we still see most of her face. Keep the facing consistent across the cast; the engine mirrors portraits so two speakers face each other. Centered bust, plain/dark background for clean compositing. Optional: a subtle hint of her bow or a quiver strap.

AVOID: GBA pixelation, low resolution, full-body, busy background, modern clothing or accessories, text, logos, watermark, over-ornate royal costume.
