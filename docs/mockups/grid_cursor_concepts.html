The Cursor in Five Tongues

Five ways the player's hand could rest upon the battlefield — each shown in the three modes the cursor actually assumes in play: idle hover, a unit selected, an enemy in the crosshairs.

✦ ✦ ✦
I
Pixel Yantra
16×16 pixel · saffron & maroon

A tight GBA-era cursor built on a strict 16-pixel grid. Four inward-opening brackets in a saffron-to-maroon ramp pulled from temple-flag dyes; tiny yantra ticks mark the cardinals, so direction reads even at 1×. Honours the existing pixel placeholder — production-cheap and Fire Emblem-literate.

II
Chakra Mandala
HD painterly · filigree ring

An ornate filigree ring slowly rotating around the tile, inspired by the mandala medallions of temple ceilings — twelve inward petals, four cardinal jewels, an inner ring of inward-pointing darts. Maximalist and reverent. Pairs best with a painted tileset, not pixels.

III
Temple Bracket
minimal cel-shaded · flat gold

Four corner brackets shaped from lotus-tipped ogee arches borrowed from temple doorway reliefs. Flat gold on transparent, with a hairline inner ghost-line doubling the frame. The most readable option at any zoom; the least committed tonally.

IV
Sanskrit Illumination
inked manuscript · cream on wood

A frame that feels inked onto palm-leaf manuscript — a dashed double-line rule broken by hand-drawn flourishes at the corners and small Devanagari-evoking glyphs at the cardinals. Storybook, scholarly, distinctly literary. Strongest when the story is told through scripture.

V
Dharma Ember
VFX-led · divine glow & particles

No outline. A warm halo of divine light settles on the tile and embers rise from it like offered incense. A dashed ring orbits silently. The most filmic, most emotional option — but also the least literal: the tile boundary is implied, not drawn.
