# Style Bible — Brief for Concept Artist

**Audience:** Concept Artist (contracted)
**You will deliver:** A complete, prescriptive Art Direction Bible (PDF + source files) that every downstream pixel artist, UI artist, and animator will use as their working contract.
**Engagement length:** 3 weeks. Possible light retainer afterward.

---

## Project context

**Project Astra** is a tactical RPG (Fire Emblem GBA-style grid combat) set in a world inspired by Indian mythology. Target platform: PC primary, Switch secondary. The bible you produce will be referenced for the prototype build *and* for the eventual vertical slice and full game — design accordingly.

---

## What you will receive on day 1

- **Mood board** (`docs/art-direction/mood-board-v1.pdf`) — the director's curated visual brief.
- **World-bible outline** — factions, themes, mythology framing (bullet-level).
- **Unit roster doc** — 4–6 player units + 5 enemies + 1 boss, with class, role, personality.
- Direct access to the director for questions, daily if needed during week 1.

---

## What you will deliver

A **single PDF (~10–15 pages)** plus all editable source files (`.psd` / `.clip`, layered).

### Required pages

| # | Page | Contents |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Cover + thesis** | One key-art piece you draw + 1–2 sentence aesthetic thesis |
| 2 | **Master palette** | 8–14 hex-coded colors (warm/cool/neutral/accent), labeled by role. Show colors in use. |
| 3 | **Character lineup** | 3–5 original characters drawn in lineup, showing class/role visual differentiation. Used to anchor proportion + costume language. |
| 4 | **Portrait standard** | Canvas size, palette, line weight, face proportions, expression range, background treatment. Include 1 finished example portrait. |
| 5 | **Map sprite standard** | Canvas size, silhouette rules, palette subset, pose convention. Include 1 finished example. |
| 6 | **Battle sprite standard** | Canvas size, frame counts for idle/attack/hit, pose convention, weapon-attachment rules. Include 1 finished example pose. |
| 7 | **Tile language** | Tile size, palette subset, biome variant rules, how trees/cliffs/water/structures interrupt the grid. Include 1 finished tile sample. |
| 8 | **Lighting rules** | Key-light direction, shadow temperature, time-of-day tonal shifts, atmospheric haze rules. |
| 9 | **UI motif** | Frame ornament language, icon style, recommended font pairing. Include 1 finished menu mockup or frame sample. |
| 10 | **Anti-rules** | "Don't do this" page — 3–5 explicit examples of mistakes that would break the style. |
| 11+ | **Source asset index** | List of every layered source file with its path. |

---

## Format requirements

- **Bible deliverable:** PDF, A4 or US Letter, portrait orientation.
- **Source files:** `.psd` or `.clip` (Clip Studio), all layers preserved and named.
- **Color values:** every color called out as **hex code**, not just "this orange."
- **Measurements:** every canvas size, line weight, and proportion stated as **pixel values**, not relative descriptions.
- **Originality:** every drawn asset must be 100% original. No traced references. No copied silhouettes from existing IP. Cultural and historical art is reference, not source material to lift from.
- **Delivery folder:** `docs/art-direction/style-bible/` with the PDF at the root and source files in `style-bible/sources/`.

---

## Process & milestones

### Week 1 — Exploration & lock

- Read mood board, world bible, unit roster.
- Produce **2–3 small style explorations** — quick key-art sketches in different interpretations of the brief.
- End of week 1: review with director. **Director picks one direction.** This is the lock point.

### Week 2 — Standards

- Refine the chosen direction into the **master palette**, **portrait standard**, and **tile language** pages.
- Mid-week check-in with director.
- Begin character lineup.

### Week 3 — Completion

- Finish character lineup, map sprite standard, battle sprite standard, lighting rules, UI motif, anti-rules.
- Compile the PDF. Deliver source files.
- Final review with director.

---

## Acceptance criteria

The bible is accepted when **all of these hold:**

- A pixel artist working from the bible alone (no further art-director input) can produce a portrait, map sprite, and tile that fit the style.
- Every recurring contractor question of the form *"what color / size / proportion / line weight should X be?"* is answered explicitly in the bible.
- The bible's anti-rules section explicitly rules out the closest "wrong" interpretation of the style.
- All assets are original IP, deliverable as layered source.
- The PDF is internally consistent — no contradictions between pages.

---

## Out of scope

- Production-ready pixel art for the full game (downstream pixel artists will produce this).
- Animation frames beyond one example pose per sprite type.
- VFX design.
- Audio.
- Marketing or packaging artwork.
- Localization considerations.

## Compensation & terms

- **Total contract value:** to be agreed between director and artist. Reference range: $1,800–$4,000 USD for the 3-week scope above.
- **Payment schedule:** 30% on signing, 30% at end of week 1 (after direction lock), 40% on final delivery.
- **IP transfer:** full assignment of all delivered work to Project Astra on final payment. Artist retains portfolio rights.
- **Revisions included:** 2 rounds of revisions per page during the 3-week window. Additional revisions billed separately.
