# Mood Board — Guide & Tasklist

**Audience:** You (the director)
**Output:** A single curated, captioned visual document (PDF) that briefs the concept artist on the game's aesthetic intent.
**Input required:** Completed reference pool from `01_REFERENCE_GATHERING.md`.
**Time budget:** 2–3 days.

---

## Purpose

Convert the raw reference pool into a **tight, opinionated, captioned brief** that the concept artist will use to produce the Style Bible. Curate, don't dump.

---

## Strategy

- **A mood board is a thesis, not a gallery.** Every image must earn its place.
- **Captions do the heavy lifting.** A captioned image directs; an uncaptioned one decorates.
- **Triangulate, don't dictate.** You're showing the *region* of aesthetic space you want, not commanding a copy.
- **Negative space matters.** Cut anything that doesn't sharpen the picture.
- **Target size: 15–30 images total.** If you have more, you haven't curated enough.

---

## Required structure

The board has **6 sections, in this order**, plus a thesis statement at the top.

### 0. Thesis statement (top of page 1)

1–2 sentences. Adjective-led. Answers: what does the player feel, what world is this, what tone?

> *Example:* "Mythic, sun-bleached, grounded. Reverent toward Indian myth without being touristic. Weight and consequence over spectacle."

### 1. Tone anchor — 1 to 3 images

The hero images that capture the feeling. Could be a film still, a painting, a key art piece. Captioned with the *quality* you want, not the subject.

### 2. Color palette — 4 to 6 images + a swatch strip

A swatch strip of 5–8 hex-coded master colors, plus reference images that exemplify those colors *in use* (not just charts).

### 3. Character style — 5 to 8 images

References for: portrait proportions, line weight, costume drape, posture, silhouette readability. Mix pixel-art and illustration references.

### 4. Environment / tile style — 4 to 6 images

References for: tile rhythm, biome palette, architectural language, landscape texture. Mix in-game tilesets and real-world architecture/landscape.

### 5. UI motif / ornament — 3 to 5 images

References for: border patterns, frame ornament, icon style, font feel. Mix textile/manuscript motifs and game UI examples.

### 6. NOT this — 1 to 2 images

The boundary-setters. Each labeled clearly with the reason it's wrong. This section is short but high-leverage — it prevents the most common misinterpretations.

---

## Caption rules

- **3–10 words per caption.** No paragraphs.
- **Specify what to extract**, not "I like this."
- Format: *"[what to extract] — [optional why]"*

| Bad caption | Good caption |
|---|---|
| "Beautiful palette" | "Ochre warmth + dust haze" |
| "FE Lyn portrait" | "Portrait scale, expression range, line weight" |
| "Stepwell" | "Geometric repetition for fort tiles" |
| "Looks cool" | "Posture and drape of cloth — dignity in pose" |

---

## Quality test

The board is done when **all of these are true:**

- [ ] If three different artists were handed this PDF with no other context, their outputs would feel like the same world.
- [ ] Every image has a caption. No exceptions.
- [ ] The thesis statement is an adjective-led sentence, not a description of mechanics.
- [ ] The "NOT this" section actually rules out at least one tempting-but-wrong direction.
- [ ] You can defend why each image is on the board (and why each one you cut is not).

If any of these fail, the board isn't ready.

---

## Format

- **Tool:** Figma frame (preferred — exports cleanly, easy to share). PureRef is fine for working but export to PDF.
- **Page size:** A3 landscape or 1920×1080, single page if possible. Two pages max.
- **Deliverable:** `docs/art-direction/mood-board-v1.pdf` + the editable source file.
- **Versioning:** if revised, bump to `v2`, `v3`. Don't overwrite.

---

## Tasklist

- [ ] Re-skim reference pool. Mark ~30 strongest candidates.
- [ ] Draft thesis statement (1–2 adjective-led sentences).
- [ ] Lay out section 1 (Tone anchor) — pick 1–3.
- [ ] Build section 2 (Palette) — swatch strip + 4–6 in-use refs. Pull hex codes.
- [ ] Build section 3 (Character style) — pick 5–8.
- [ ] Build section 4 (Environment) — pick 4–6.
- [ ] Build section 5 (UI motif) — pick 3–5.
- [ ] Build section 6 (NOT this) — pick 1–2 with explicit reason labels.
- [ ] Caption every single image (3–10 words).
- [ ] Sleep on it. Re-read next morning. Cut at least 2 weak images.
- [ ] Run the quality test (above). Fix any failing item.
- [ ] Export to PDF as `mood-board-v1.pdf`.
- [ ] Save editable source alongside the PDF.
