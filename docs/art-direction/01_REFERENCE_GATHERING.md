# Reference Gathering — Guide & Tasklist

**Audience:** You (the director)
**Output:** A tagged pool of 80–120 raw visual references that will later be curated into a mood board.
**Time budget:** 3–5 days, focused.

---

## Purpose

Collect a wide pool of visual material from which the mood board will be curated. **This is the raw material, not the deliverable.** Wide net now, sharp curation later.

---

## Strategy

- **Quantity over polish at this stage.** You're prospecting, not building.
- **Mix in-genre and out-of-genre.** Out-of-genre is what prevents your game from looking like a clone.
- **Skew toward real-world/cultural sources** — paintings, architecture, textiles. These are the well others aren't drawing from.
- **Save the source URL** with every image. You'll need it later to credit/avoid IP issues.
- **No AI-generated images.** They poison the well with statistical averages of training data.

---

## Sources to mine

| Category | Where to look | Target count |
|---|---|---|
| **In-genre tactical RPGs** | Fire Emblem GBA trilogy, Tactics Ogre, Final Fantasy Tactics, Vagrant Story, Triangle Strategy, Unicorn Overlord, Disgaea | 20+ |
| **Indian classical art** | Rajput miniatures, Mughal manuscripts, Pahari paintings, Tanjore, Pattachitra, Kalighat, Madhubani, Amar Chitra Katha covers | 20+ |
| **Architecture** | Hampi, Khajuraho, Chand Baori, Rani ki Vav, Mehrangarh, Jaisalmer, Hoysala temples, North Indian forts | 10+ |
| **Landscape** | Himalayan foothills, Deccan plateau, Thar desert, Western Ghats, river valleys, monsoon skies | 10+ |
| **Textile / ornament** | Pattu silk borders, brocade patterns, block prints, temple carvings, manuscript margins | 10+ |
| **Out-of-genre game UI / icons** | Hades, Slay the Spire, Inscryption, Hollow Knight — for UI motif inspiration only | 5+ |
| **Anti-references** | Things that look superficially right but are wrong for your game (e.g., Bollywood-glossy modern, generic "mystical India" stock, anime-stylized FE Awakening) | 5+ |

## What to collect *within* an image

Don't think "save the picture." Think "save the picture for one specific reason." Possible reasons:

- A **palette** (color relationships)
- A **lighting condition** (golden hour, monsoon overcast, torchlight)
- A **silhouette** (character pose, environmental landmark)
- A **composition** (how elements are framed)
- An **ornament/pattern** (border, repeating motif)
- A **texture** (parchment, weathered stone, dyed cloth)

You don't have to caption yet. But when you skim later, you should remember why each made the cut.

---

## Organization

- **Tool:** PureRef (preferred — infinite canvas, fast) or Figma board.
- **Tagging:** at minimum, tag each image with its source category (e.g., `in-genre`, `architecture`, `textile`).
- **Anti-refs go in their own zone**, clearly marked.
- **No captions yet.** That's mood-board work.
- **File:** save the working file at `docs/art-direction/reference-pool.pur` (or `.fig`).

---

## When to stop

Stop when **any of these is true:**

- You've hit ~80–120 images across the source mix.
- Adding new references stops changing your sense of the game's look.
- You've spent 5 days on this. Hard cap.

You will be tempted to keep gathering. Don't. The mood board phase will reveal what's missing — you can top up then.
