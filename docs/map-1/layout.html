Map 1 — The Village Lane

The simple teaching map. Title screen → end of Map 1 in ≤15 min · resolves in ~4 turns · teaches movement, phases, attack, dodge, and the climactic critical hit — all through the fiction, no tutorial screens.

The idea: a vertical threat gradient

The map reads south = safe, north = danger, and Aranya's whole arc is a bottom-to-top push.

South — the homes. Huts and villagers at the bottom edge: what she's protecting. It's literally behind her when she plants her feet.
Middle — the commons. Open ground where the first Rakshasa is already bearing down on the homes. The contested space.
North — the forest mouth. The dark treeline the Rakshasas spilled from, where the stationary mini-boss waits. The trees pinch inward — the climax happens in a hemmed-in space, so it feels cornered.

She arrives home to a monster at the doorstep (defend — max urgency), then carries the fight back into the woods to finish the threat (offense — growing nerve). Her direction of travel is her arc.

The four turns

Turn 1

Movement

Teaches grid movement & phases. Aranya dashes from the east lane to the purple intercept tile, planting herself between the Rakshasa and the homes. On the enemy phase it closes in — now in range. "I need to get near the village before the Rakshasas do."
Turn 2

Attack + Dodge

Combat-screen debut. She attacks at range 1; the Rakshasa survives and counters — and she dodges (hard-coded), teaching that attacks can miss. She sits at ~60–70% HP.
Turn 3

Finish + free move

Freedom opens. She finishes the first Rakshasa, the purple guidance drops, and she pushes north up the center clearing toward the forest. "The village seems safe — but there's another one up north."
Turn 4

Cornered climax

The critical hit. She initiates on the stationary mini-boss, hemmed by trees. Its counter drops her to ~2 HP — then she dodges and lands a guaranteed crit (×3) to win. The Blazing-Sword "about to die, but I pulled it off" beat.

Calls I made — flip any

  • Size 11×10. Big enough that the intercept race and the north push feel like two distinct movements. Could compress to ~9×9 for a tighter Lyn-mode feel, at some cost to the race tension.
  • Entry: south-east lane. Matches the design doc's "from the right." Alternative is due-east-center — minor.
  • No mechanical terrain. Trees and huts are pure framing/passability — per the locked "no terrain bonuses on Map 1." A decorative stream or dirt path could be added for flavor only.

One open question before I go further

Are the villagers targetable units the Rakshasas can reach and kill (a real fail-state), or set-dressing? The objective "no villager is hurt" implies the former — which means the first Rakshasa's path must credibly threaten a specific villager so the intercept feels earned. That changes exactly where the enemy and homes sit.
