Proposal v2 · playbook-applied

Map 1 — The Bridge at Suvarnapur

Same locked goals as v1 (≤15 min · ~4 turns · teach movement, phases, attack, dodge, crit) — but every choice now traces to the SRPG Prototype Design Playbook. The headline change: the map is built around protecting a person the enemy is visibly racing toward, with enemy intent telegraphed from turn 1.

Headline principle — telegraph enemy intent §1 #1

The playbook's single highest-leverage Act-1 decision: every enemy's intent must be readable in 2–3 seconds. v1 had none. In v2 the raider shows a red intent path from turn 1 — it is heading across the bridge for a specific villager. The player reads the threat and the correct move (cork the bridge) becomes self-evident §2 "correct move = obvious move". No tutorial popup required.

The idea: hold the line, then take the fight back

A stream divides the map. The village lives on the south bank; the Rakshasas spilled from the forest to the north. One footbridge connects them — the only way across.

Defend (turns 1–2). A raider is charging the bridge to reach the villagers. Aranya races to cork the bridge — one tile decides whether the village bleeds. This is a protect / defensive objective, not "kill all" §1 #4§6 win-conditions: every turn is under pressure because the enemy is racing a target, not waiting to be hunted.

Pursue (turns 3–4). Raider down, she crosses the bridge north into the forest mouth to end the mini-boss that's been looming. Her bottom-to-top push is her arc: shield the home, then carry the fight to them.

The four turns

Turn 1

Read the telegraph · Move

Teaches movement + reading intent §1 #1§2 Lyn Ch.1. The raider's red path points across the bridge at the villager. Aranya dashes to cork the bridge's south foot. Enemy phase: the raider charges onto the bridge — now blocked and in range. "Not one more. They take no one else."
Turn 2

Attack + Dodge

Combat-screen debut. She strikes at range 1 across the bridge — her 1–2 range means she can trade up close, so the duel is two-directional §8 #2 lord identity. The raider counters and she dodges (hard-coded), teaching evasion.
Turn 3

Finish · cross · pursue

Freedom opens. Raider down, the south bank is safe, the purple guide drops. She crosses the bridge north toward the looming mini-boss §4 Act 1 ends by changing the objective.
Turn 4

Cornered crit

The signature beat. She initiates on the stationary boss, hemmed by trees; its counter drops her to ~2 HP, then she dodges and lands a guaranteed crit ×3 to win — the Lyn-vs-Batta scripted crit §4 Act 1, the lord's identity made unmissable §1 #10.

How v2 differs from v1 — and the one tradeoff

Read this before choosing between the two

  • v1 was an open intercept-then-hunt: get near the village, kill the advancing raider, walk north, kill the boss. No enemy telegraph, no concrete person at risk, no chokepoint.
  • v2 is a telegraphed protect-the-bridge map: a named threat racing a visible victim, a single legible defensive tile, then the pursuit. It scores far higher against the playbook (telegraph, defensive objective, obvious-correct-move, narrative unity).
  • The tradeoff: v2 introduces a soft chokepoint (the footbridge). Your design doc explicitly reserves chokepoints for Map 2. v2's bridge is fiction-justified (a stream crossing, not a taught "defensive-formation" system) and needs no tutorial — but it does put a chokepoint on screen earlier than the doc planned. If you want Map 1 to stay strictly chokepoint-free, we keep v1's open field and just bolt on the telegraph + protect-objective from v2.
