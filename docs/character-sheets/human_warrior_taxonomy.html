Project Astra · Character Design Reference · Vol. 01

A Field Atlas of Human Warriors

Nine independent classification trees, drawn from the great epics, for inventing original heroes by combination rather than imitation.

How to read this atlas

Each warrior in the source material can be located along nine separate axes — who they were born as, how powerful they are, what they fight with, what binds them, and so on. The trees below treat these axes as independent dimensions: pick one branch from any number of trees, and the intersection becomes a fresh, mythologically grounded character seed.

No real names appear in the visible text — only the structural shapes. The goal is inspiration by combination, not reconstruction. A "Liminal-born Mace fighter on Elephant, Tier C astras, bound by a Vengeance Vow, of Rakshasa lineage, war-hardened, and a Tragic Loyalist" is not a real figure from any epic — but every part of it is.

Part One

Nine Trees of Classification

Each tree sorts warriors along one axis. Read them as independent — the same warrior occupies one branch on every tree at once.

01

Birth-Class & Legitimacy — Varna

By whose authority does this warrior take up arms?

  1. Pure Warrior-Caste Established

    Born into the warrior estate. Authority to fight is hereditary and unquestioned; the institutions of war are theirs by right.

    Mechanic+command respect, +access to standing armies, low identity friction

  2. Priestly-Born Warrior Sacred & Lethal

    Born to a priestly lineage but trained in arms. Carries scriptural knowledge, which is the gateway to the highest divine weapons — but also the heaviest moral load.

    Mechanichighest astra-tier ceiling, internal dharma conflict, gated by ritual purity

  3. Liminal / Mixed Birth In-Between

    Born across castes, raised outside their birth-class, or of disputed parentage. Excluded from establishment training; their power, when it comes, is hard-won and resented.

    Mechanicexclusion-fueled stat boost, identity-based questlines, unconventional training paths

  4. Royalty / Dynastic Heir Throne-Bound

    A warrior-caste subtype where the throne itself constrains personal will. Strategic decisions echo through entire kingdoms; private morality is rarely affordable.

    Mechanicinstitutional vows by default, −personal autonomy, +strategic influence on map-wide events

02

Power Tier — Rathi-class hierarchy

How many lesser warriors does this one equal in open battle?

  1. Foot Soldier (Padatik) Tier 0 · Baseline

    The unit of measurement. Everything else is counted in multiples of these.

    Combat Value1× — the standard against which all others scale

  2. Cavalry (Ashvarohi) Tier 0.5

    Mounted skirmisher. Mobility and reach lift them above the infantry baseline.

    Combat Value~12× foot soldier; +redeploy speed, +flank pressure

  3. Elephant Warrior (Gaja) Tier 1

    A war-elephant rider. Elevated platform, crushing mass, psychological dominance.

    Combat Value~12× cavalry; +morale-break aura, +line-of-sight, slow but hard to stop

  4. Half-Charioteer (ArdhaRathi) Tier 2

    A capable chariot-warrior, not yet a champion. Equals one elephant unit in open field.

    Combat Value~1× Gaja; competent ranged platform

  5. Charioteer (Rathi) Tier 3

    A battle-hardened chariot champion; mid-tier kings and seasoned officers fall here. The lower bound of "named warrior."

    Combat Value~12× ArdhaRathi / ~5,000 foot soldiers

  6. Master-Charioteer (Atirathi) Tier 4

    Multi-weapon master; a single unit can stall an army's advance.

    Combat Value~12× Rathi / ~60,000 foot soldiers

  7. Great Charioteer (Maharathi) Tier 5 · Champion

    Master of every form of combat known. A presence on the battlefield freezes lesser units in place.

    Combat Value~12× Atirathi / ~720,000 foot soldiers; battlefield-defining

  8. Supreme-Great Charioteer (Ati-Maharathi) Tier 6 · Rare

    A super-warrior; only a handful exist in any given age. Capable of holding the line against multiple Maharathis.

    Combat Value~12× Maharathi; appearance alone shifts war's course

  9. Transcendent Warrior (Maha-Maharathi) Tier 7 · Near-Divine

    Beyond mortal scale. Effectively unkillable by conventional means; usually a divine incarnation in disguise.

    Combat Valuenarrative cap; cannot be defeated mechanically — only restrained by oath, vow, or self-limitation

03

Weapon Specialization — Shastra-yuddha

What does the warrior reach for first?

  1. Bow Specialist (Dhanurdhari) Long Range

    The archetypal warrior of the epics. Primary weapon is the great bow; the bow is also the conduit through which most divine weapons are launched.

    Mechanichigh single-target damage, range advantage, primary astra-delivery vehicle

  2. Mace / Club Fighter (Gadayudha) Brute Force

    Iron mace specialist. Strength-based, medium range, devastating in close. Often paired with low astra-access — the mace fighter trusts the body, not the mantra.

    Mechanichigh AoE / cleave damage, strength-scaling, lower astra ceiling

  3. Sword Fighter (Asi-yuddha) Close Quarters

    Twin-sword and single-blade specialists. High mobility, fast technique; tier compensated by skill rather than scale.

    Mechanichigh mobility & counter-attack, tech-scaled rather than stat-scaled

  4. Spear / Lance Fighter (Shaktayudha) Mid Range

    Pole-arm and javelin specialists. Reach, control, and the rare one-throw kill — the spear is also the form most divine "shakti" weapons take.

    Mechanicreach control, +zone-of-denial, single-use ranged shakti weapons

  5. Axe Warrior (Parasha-yuddha) Cleaving

    Battle-axe specialists. High-momentum, often tied to a specific signature artifact; rare style associated with divinely-chosen warriors.

    Mechanichigh momentum / charge damage, often bound to a unique artifact

  6. Multi-Weapon Generalist All Forms

    Master of every shastra. Indicates extreme experience or elite training; their flexibility is their advantage.

    Mechanic+tactical flexibility, +adapts to enemy archetype, requires high power-tier

  7. Astra-Specialist / Warrior-Sage Mantric

    Primary "weapon" is the recited mantra rather than steel. Their power lives in memory and ritual; physical prowess is secondary.

    Mechanic+highest astra-tier, −physical durability, vulnerable to memory/concentration disruption

  8. Unique / Signature Weapon One-of-a-Kind

    Defined by a single weapon no one else can wield — three infallible arrows, a thousand-armed style, a cursed bow that only fires once. Power is bound to that artifact alone.

    Mechanicextreme situational power, finite uses, identity collapses if weapon is lost

04

Mount & Transport — Vahana

From what platform does the warrior fight?

  1. On Foot (Padati) Infantry

    Unencumbered. Lowest profile on the battlefield, easiest to hide in formation, hardest to single out.

    Mechanicunrestricted terrain, +stealth in crowd, low durability

  2. Horse-Mounted (Ashvarohi) Cavalry

    Rapid redeploy, skirmish-and-withdraw, flank exploitation. The greatest horsemen could deflect raindrops with a sword in mid-gallop.

    Mechanic+movement range, +flanking damage, vulnerable when dismounted

  3. Elephant-Mounted (Gaja) Heavy Mount

    High elevation, AoE presence, area denial. The elephant warrior dominates archer positioning by sight and space alike.

    Mechanic+line-of-sight bonus, +morale aura, slow movement, hard counter to massed infantry

  4. Chariot-Borne (Ratha) Two-Person Platform

    The signature platform of elite warriors: a mobile archery deck operated by an archer and a charioteer. Effectively a stationary cannon once positioned — and useless if the wheel sticks.

    Mechanicranged platform with armor, requires 2-unit crew, immobile once committed; charioteer's morale affects archer's accuracy

05

Divine Weapon Access — Astra-tier

How high in the mantric arsenal can this warrior reach?

  1. Tier A — Elemental Common

    The first tier any trained warrior masters: fire, water, wind, rain, lightning. Each is countered by its elemental opposite, producing readable rock-paper-scissors duels.

    Mechanicbroadly accessible, low cost, hard-counterable by opposite element

  2. Tier B — Indra-class & Illusion Restricted

    Mid-tier astras: mass-stupefaction, sleep-inducing, illusion-weaving, weather-bending. Crowd control rather than raw damage.

    Mechanicbattlefield CC, temporary disables, useful against numerically superior foes

  3. Tier C — Brahmastra-class High Cosmic

    Single-target cosmic damage. Must be invoked AND recalled; an unrecalled astra continues to consume the world. Counters all of Tier A and most of Tier B.

    Mechanicmassive damage; recall mantra is a separate piece of knowledge — without it, victory becomes catastrophe

  4. Tier D — Apocalyptic World-Ending

    Brahmashirastra, Narayanastra, Pashupatastra-tier weapons. Capable of unmaking armies, kingdoms, or worlds. Hard constraints: cannot target mortals, cannot be used twice, only one warrior per generation may know it.

    Mechanicnarrative-scale damage; locked behind use-restrictions, target-restrictions, or single-use limits — a tool the player almost never deploys

06

Vows & Binding Constraints — Vrata, Shapa

What can this warrior not do, and what does that cost them?

  1. Institutional Vow To a Throne

    A binding oath of duty to a kingdom, dynasty, or office — even when its cause is morally wrong. The most tragic warriors of the epics fight on the wrong side because of this vow.

    Mechanic+institutional authority & resources, −autonomy; breaking the vow grants new powers but inflicts permanent moral collapse

  2. Weapon-Use Constraint Conditional Lockout

    A specific weapon cannot be used under specific conditions: never against mortals, never twice in a war, never on someone unarmed. The most powerful tool in the warrior's hand is also the most situational.

    Mechanichighest-damage ability gated by enemy-type, count, or context; using it against the rules backfires

  3. Ascetic Vow Renunciation

    Voluntary surrender of food, sleep, comfort, or family for a fixed period in exchange for power. The body becomes hollow but the will becomes a weapon.

    Mechanic+stat boosts during the vow, −quality-of-life mechanics; vow ends with ritual release

  4. Vengeance Vow Time-Bounded

    A sworn oath to slay a specific target — often by sunset, often or else. Failure costs the warrior's own life by self-immolation.

    Mechanicturn-limited objective, +damage vs target, instant-death failure state

  5. Algorithmic / Paradoxical Vow Self-Defeating

    A vow whose logic eats itself: "I will always fight for the weaker side" — but the moment they join, that side becomes stronger. The warrior is a walking contradiction, often resolved by self-sacrifice.

    Mechanicunpredictable allegiance flips; AI may switch sides mid-battle; plot-locked self-sacrifice ending

  6. Curse of Forgetting Conditional Failure

    Cursed by a slighted teacher or sage: at the moment of greatest need, the warrior forgets the mantra they most need. Power present, access denied.

    Mechanicastra invocation fails under contextual stress (HP threshold, story flag, specific enemy); not random — triggered

  7. Mortality Denial Death-Choice

    A boon that defers death until the warrior chooses it. Effectively unkillable while the will holds — but the moment they accept death, it is final and without delay.

    Mechaniccannot be reduced below 1 HP unless flagged "willing"; decision is permanent

07

Origin & Lineage — Janma

What blood, what fire, or what god fathered this warrior?

  1. Pure Mortal Human Through

    No divine parent, no miraculous birth. Whatever they achieve is achieved by training, will, and luck. The truest underdogs.

    Mechanicbaseline; +growth-curve bonus from training events; no innate ceiling

  2. Demigod / Semi-Divine Divine Parent

    One mortal parent, one divine parent. The divine domain (sun, wind, storm, sky, river) imprints a starting bonus and often a destined fate.

    Mechanic+innate stat in divine domain, +divine boon flexibility, often +extended lifespan

  3. Rakshasa-Hybrid Half-Demonic

    Half-blood of demonic or non-human kin. Carries access to sorcery, illusion, and night-combat advantages — but also instinctive prejudice from the human world.

    Mechanic+sorcery skill, +night-combat bonus, demonic weapon access; social penalty in human settlements

  4. Avatar / Incarna God in Mortal Form

    A full divine incarnation, voluntarily limited to mortal form. The defining arc is self-restraint: choosing not to deploy the cosmic power they obviously possess.

    Mechanicnarrative power-cap (self-imposed); rare unlocks where the divine surface punctures through; cannot be balanced — only restrained

  5. Ritual-Born / Fire-Born Born of Sacrifice

    Born not of a womb but of ceremony — emerged fully formed from sacrificial fire, often summoned for a specific purpose (to kill a specific person, to fulfill a specific prophecy).

    Mechanicbegins at high tier with full equipment; locked into the destiny they were summoned for; identity = mission

  6. Immortal (Chiranjivi) Cursed Endless

    Cannot die of age or normal wounds. The text is explicit — this is punishment, not blessing. Watching loved ones die forever, motivation often becomes ending the curse rather than extending life.

    Mechanicunkillable until specific narrative condition met; goal = release; recurring presence across multiple game eras

08

Training Tradition — Guru-parampara

From whom did the knowledge come, and at what cost?

  1. Avatar-Lineage Highest Pedigree

    Direct training under a divine incarnation. Unlocks the highest tier of astras and the most ferocious martial techniques. Few in any age can claim this.

    Mechanic+Tier C minimum astra access, accelerated learning, +divine-artifact compatibility

  2. Institutional Archery School Establishment

    Trained at a recognized institution under a master. Knowledge is unevenly transferred — favorites learn the recall mantras, the rest do not. Same school, different ceilings.

    Mechanicinstitutional bias mechanic: same class, different ceilings; favoritism flag affects access

  3. Self-Taught / Idol-Worship Outside the System

    Excluded from formal training, the warrior teaches themselves — sometimes through devotion to a clay idol of the master they were denied. Slow start, eventual parity.

    Mechanic−early game stats, +late-game ceiling = trained warrior; vulnerable to formal-school curse mechanics

  4. Divine-Direct Teaching Personal Tutelage

    A god or sage takes the warrior personally as a student — often during a year of exile in the celestial realm. Compressed time, accelerated mastery, multiple astras per training arc.

    Mechanicrapid astra-learning during specific story arcs; tied to divine favor (revocable)

  5. Martial-Body Tradition Hand-to-Hand Origin

    Trained in the unarmed and weapons-of-the-body traditions said to be taught by the destroyer god himself. Philosophical combat — the body is the weapon, the breath is the discipline.

    Mechanic+unarmed combat bonus, +counter-throw & grapple, weapon-loss does not disable

  6. Apprenticeship with Guru-Dakshina Knowledge for Sacrifice

    Formal student-teacher bond ending with a "fee" demanded by the master — never gold, always something irreversible: a thumb, a head, a lifelong silence. Knowledge bought with permanent loss.

    Mechanicfull skill access at the cost of a permanent stat-loss or limb-loss; cannot be undone

  7. War-Hardened (No Formal Master) Forged in Battle

    No guru, no school. Power comes from surviving more wars than most live to see one of. Solid mid-tier ceiling; locked out of the highest astras.

    Mechanic+Rathi-tier minimum, −astra ceiling, +battle-specific bonuses, +veteran morale aura

09

Dharma Alignment — not Good / Evil

How does this warrior resolve the conflict between duty, honor, and cosmic order?

  1. Dharma-Bound Righteous & Self-Limiting

    Operates strictly within the rules of righteous combat. Will not strike the unarmed, will not lie, will not abandon a vow even when the vow is killing them. Gains divine blessing while truthful — loses it the moment they slip.

    Mechanicpassive divine blessing (e.g. invulnerable wheels, true-strike), revoked permanently on first vow-violation

  2. Adharma-Exploiter Rule-Bender

    Wins by bending rules others won't. Strikes at night, strikes the unarmed, weaponizes ceremonies. Each transgression buys an immediate edge — and a permanent debt.

    Mechanic+temporary action economy (extra turn, astra recovery), accumulating curse debt; allies defect on witnessing transgression

  3. Strategic-Neutral Unbound

    Fights for outcomes, not for righteousness. May refuse to bear arms personally yet shape entire wars by counsel. No vow-binding; maximum tactical flexibility.

    Mechanic+tactical flexibility, no divine blessing or curse, +mercenary-loyalty & recruitment options

  4. Tragic Loyalist Right Person, Wrong Side

    Knows perfectly well which side is righteous — and fights for the other one anyway, out of personal loyalty, gratitude, or love. The most heartbreaking shape a warrior can take.

    Mechanicinternal-conflict dialogue branches; if forced to confront the truth, may refuse to fight key targets; hidden recruitment route

  5. Corrective Vengeance Dharmic Rage

    Fights to restore cosmic balance, even if methods are extreme. Not "evil" but not gentle — these warriors wage cycles of annihilation against entire classes of people who have failed dharma.

    Mechanic+bonus damage vs designated target-class, burn-out timer, immortal curse if mission unfulfilled at death

Part Two

Signature Combinations

Six cross-cutting archetypes the source material keeps producing — proof that certain branch-intersections create characters more compelling than the sum of their parts. Use as seed templates, then perturb.

— Combination 01 —

The Cursed Apocalypse

World-ending power held by someone morally compromised or structurally destined to fail. The most dangerous tool in the wrong hands — but the hands cannot let go.

GameplayLate-game power spike that always risks backfiring. Massive damage potential, equally massive self-destruction probability. Players will save them for one critical moment — and they may still misfire.

— Combination 02 —

The Bound Commander

The right person, on the wrong side, by an unbreakable institutional vow. Fully knows their cause is unjust. Fights anyway, because the oath is older than the war.

GameplayUltimate morale anchor for the enemy. Grants passive blessings to allies; can break under specific moral pressure events. If their vow snaps, they gain new powers and a permanent curse.

— Combination 03 —

The Insulted Demigod

Born low, gifted high. Excluded from establishment training despite carrying divine blood. Resentment is fuel; every insult charges the next victory.

GameplayUnderdog scaling — gains stat boosts from being insulted, excluded, or underestimated. Cursed by establishment figures who recognize them too late.

— Combination 04 —

The Reluctant Annihilator

Priestly birth, warrior body, mantric specialist. Understands the spiritual weight of the destruction they can call down — and is therefore the warrior most reluctant to use it.

GameplayTier C/D astra access from session one, but moral cost on every use. Can refuse to deploy (morale cost) or deploy and accumulate karma debt that compounds across the campaign.

— Combination 05 —

The Devoted Second

Loves and serves someone greater, will never surpass them — and could have, in another life. The tragedy is the gap between capability and ceiling, both freely chosen.

GameplayExceptional support unit. Massive bonuses while adjacent to or fighting for their chosen leader; near-zero solo-win condition; high probability of self-sacrifice trigger.

— Combination 06 —

The Held-Back Nuke

Carries the most powerful tool available — and is forbidden from using it under almost every condition. Single-use, target-restricted, vow-locked. The win-button you spend the campaign refusing to press.

GameplayOne guaranteed-kill ability locked behind heavy restrictions. Using it auto-wins the encounter but breaks character arc or violates a vow — narrative cost is permanent and irreversible.

Part Three

Genre Guardrails — Don't Lose These

Ten mechanical signatures that make Indian-mythological warriors structurally different from generic fantasy. When inventing characters, check that your design preserves these — or breaks them deliberately, not by accident.

  1. Astras are Learned, Not Looted

    Mantric weapons are knowledge, transmitted by guru or earned by penance. They are never random treasure. This means astra access is a design-time gating decision, expressed through mentor relationships and training trees, not loot tables.

  2. Guru-Dakshina — Asymmetric Knowledge Cost

    Completing training demands a "fee" set by the teacher. It is never money. It is always something precious and irreversible — a thumb, a head, a name, a lifelong silence. Knowledge has a permanent body-cost.

  3. Dharma is Mechanically Rewarded & Punished

    Truthfulness grants tangible passive bonuses (a chariot whose wheels never touch ground). A single half-lie revokes them — permanently, not until next save. Morality is not flavor; it is mechanics.

  4. Ultra-Weapons Have Hard Constraints

    Every Tier-D weapon comes with a structural lockout: cannot be used twice, cannot target mortals, cannot be used after surrender, must be recalled or it consumes forever. There is no single "win button" that lacks a leash.

  5. Power Tier Scales Exponentially

    A Maharathi is not 4× a Rathi — they are roughly 720,000× a foot soldier. Tier-matching is therefore critical: sending a low-tier unit at a high-tier one is suicide by design, and the player must feel it.

  6. Immortality is the Curse

    The warriors who cannot die are presented as the most pitied. Their motivation is release, not life-extension. Treat immortality as a failure state; design endings that earn it back.

  7. Alignment Drives Allies, Not Damage

    Dharma-bound warriors attract loyal companions; rule-benders attract mercenaries and defectors. Alignment is a faction/recruitment mechanic, not a personal stat curve.

  8. The Chariot is a Two-Person Platform

    Archer plus charioteer. The charioteer's morale, taunting, or skill directly affects the archer's accuracy. Separate them and both are vulnerable. A chariot warrior cannot be a solo unit.

  9. Mantras Forget Under Stress

    The defining curse of one of the greatest archers: at the moment he most needed his astras, the recall mantras vanished from memory. Not a random miss — a triggered failure under contextual pressure (a stuck wheel, an emotional collision, a story flag).

  10. Vow-Breaking is Permanent

    A vow-bound benefit is a locked-in mechanical advantage. Breaking the vow removes it forever; the warrior does not earn it back. This is the price of the bargain.
