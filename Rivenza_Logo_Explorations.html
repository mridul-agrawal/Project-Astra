Eight marks
for a studio
in waiting.

Concept directions for the corporate identity of a craft-led game studio, beginning with Project Astra.

The mark should age well, accrue meaning through a body of work, and carry a quiet Indian sensibility without leaning on stereotype.

None of these are final. The right answer should become visible only after the wrong ones.

Eight directions One studio Pre-launch
The Directions I — VIII
I. The First Ink Drop — Pooled
rvenza Technologies

A single hand-painted dot replaces the tittle of the i. The studio's first ink-drop on a clean page; every game shipped is another drop in the same hand.

II. The First Ink Drop — Bloom
rvenza Technologies

Same gesture, allowed to bleed. A faint halo surrounds the brush mark — ink hitting fibrous paper. Warmer and more handmade than I; reads as just-made.

III. The Vāstu Glyph
Rivenza Technologies

The skeleton of a vāstu purusha mandala — a 4×4 grid with the central brahma sthāna marked solid. Reads as Vignelli-clean to a Western eye; reads as yantra to anyone who knows.

IV. The Colophon

Treats Rivenza as a publisher's imprint — a sealed colophon in the lineage of Penguin Classics, NYRB, the Modern Library. Signals: we publish games for the long haul.

V. The Devanagari R
Rivenza

A Latin R built on Devanagari structural DNA — a top bar that overhangs the stem like a shirorekha, a more angular bowl, a wedge-cut leg. Latin to read, Indian to construct.

VI. The Display Wordmark
Rivenza
Technologies

No symbol. The wordmark alone, set in a heavy display serif — Fraunces at its most monumental. In the lineage of A24, Criterion, Annapurna. Restraint as the statement.

VII. The Inscribed
Rivenza
technologies · est. MMXXVI

Lettering pressed into the surface — light from above catching the lower edge of each carve. FromSoftware energy without iconography. Reads as ancient, but is just type and shadow.

VIII. The Counterpoint
Rivenza
Technologies

A mark-led counterpoint to the typography-led directions: a heavy black block with the R carved out as paper. Sculptural, modern, confident — Crouwel meets Pentagram.
